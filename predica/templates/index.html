Housing Insecurity

Housing Affordability

The U.S. was facing a crisis-level housing affordability issue, especially for low-income households, even before the coronavirus pandemic. In 2018, a quarter of renter households — 10.9 million — were spending more than half of their income on rent. When the pandemic hit, its impacts were felt more strongly by households with lower incomes, by renters and by people of color.

Housing as a burden on households.

Housing is considered a burden on a household when it costs more than 30% of the household’s income, leaving less money available for groceries, transportation, childcare and bills. Beyond that, communities where people spend more than 32% of their income on rent can expect a more rapid increase in homelessness.

In 2019, 37.1 million households were “housing cost burdened,” spending 30% or more of their income on housing, representing 30.2% of all households nationwide. One in seven households — 17.6 million in total — were “severely cost burdened,” spending half or more of their income on housing.

Challenge

How can data be used to provide early warnings of housing vulnerability, both for individuals and community groups, to avoid evictions and homelessness?

The smart move to take hold of the future.

Summary

Predica is an intelligent tool that utilizes current data trends in the housing and job sector to predict housing security for a user. It also seeks to provide actionable steps that can possibly reduce the user’s housing insecurity or mitigate any challenges that the user may face because of their situation. It also seeks to provide these predictions in a timely fashion that enables the user to be constantly aware of where they stand when it comes to housing security.

Our Services

Predica Score

We use your data together with current data trends in the housing and job sector to predict your housing security. We seek to give you an approximation of where you stand given your current situation.

Insights

Our goal is not just to give you an approximate score, but we also seek to give you actionable steps and insights that can help you stay informed about your future and how you can prepare for it.

Personalized Alerts

We give you the power to decide how and when you would like to receive notifications about changes in your predica score for a specific period in a specific area.
